# Final Business Requirements

## Purpose

Provide a reusable acceptance gate for business requirements that are ready to control design, implementation, testing, or release.

## Context

This file separates accepted business requirements from early requirement discovery. Do not mark a requirement published unless its source, scope, acceptance evidence, and owner are clear.

## Acceptance Gate

| Gate | Requirement |
|---|---|---|---|
| Source | Requirement has an approved source or explicit user decision. |
| Scope | In-scope and out-of-scope behavior is clear. |
| Acceptance | Acceptance criteria or verification gap is recorded. |
| Trace | Requirement links to use case, BPMN, contract, QA, or architecture as needed. |
| Owner | Business owner or approval owner is known or explicitly `unknown`. |

## Business Requirement Record Shape

| Field | Description |
|---|---|
| ID | Stable business requirement identifier. |
| Requirement | Accepted business behavior or constraint. |
| Source | Decision, ticket, requirement draft, or stakeholder approval. |
| Acceptance Evidence | AC, test, scenario, review, or human approval. |
| Status | Draft, reviewed, published, deprecated. |

## Starter Index

| ID | Requirement | Acceptance Evidence | Status |
|---|---|---|---|
| BR-000 | Business requirement acceptance framework is available. | Documentation review | draft |

## Promotion Rules

Promote a requirement here only when it should guide downstream design, implementation, testing, or release. Keep drafts in `00.requirement.md` until accepted.
